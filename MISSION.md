# SOLP Mission

## Sudanese Open Language Project

### Mission

The mission of the Sudanese Open Language Project (SOLP) is to build, maintain, and openly share high-quality language resources for Sudanese Arabic while establishing a scalable foundation for documenting all languages spoken in Sudan.

SOLP is committed to preserving Sudan's linguistic heritage by collecting, documenting, organizing, and publishing reliable language resources according to scientific and open standards.

The project aims to support education, linguistic research, software development, language learning, and artificial intelligence by providing openly accessible datasets and tools that can be freely used by researchers, developers, educators, students, and communities.

To achieve this mission, SOLP focuses on:

- Collecting and documenting Sudanese Arabic vocabulary and expressions.
- Building open digital dictionaries and lexical resources.
- Developing structured language corpora.
- Documenting regional dialects and linguistic variations.
- Creating annotated datasets for Natural Language Processing (NLP).
- Building speech, OCR, handwriting, and translation datasets.
- Supporting academic research and technological innovation.
- Encouraging collaboration from researchers, developers, linguists, and native speakers.
- Promoting open data and open-source principles.
- Expanding gradually to include all languages of Sudan.

SOLP is not a commercial product or a single application. It is an open, community-driven language infrastructure designed to preserve Sudanese languages and enable future technologies built upon reliable language resources.
